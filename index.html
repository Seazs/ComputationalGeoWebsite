Computational Geometry - Shape Interpolation

Theory

The Hausdorff Middle is a method for interpolating between two shapes by finding a point that minimizes the Hausdorff distance between the two shapes. This technique is useful in computational geometry for shape analysis and morphing.

The Hausdorff distance is defined as the maximum distance of a set to the nearest point in another set. The Hausdorff Middle finds a point that balances the distances from both shapes.
