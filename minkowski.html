Computational Geometry - Minkowski Sum of Shapes

🎯 Theoretical Concept

The minkowski sum of sets A and B is defined as .

A⊕︎B={a + b | a ∈ A, b ∈ B}

This looks like this:

Intuitively, The Minkowski sum can be seen as running a shape along another, and the "trace" left behind is the resulting set (but it's a very simplified view and the terms are not exact).
